Project Overview

For my Design Studio II project, I explored the use of public spaces in Chinatown by street vendors and local retailers utilizing temporary furniture. The project focuses on repurposing waste plastics and fabrics from nearby businesses to create a new type of public furniture.

The versatility and lightweight nature of these materials allow visitors to "build and stack" the furniture according to their needs. I envision this project taking place every weekend, in collaboration with Think! Chinatown. By involving the community, the project aims to foster a sense of ownership and create a meaningful experience.

Concept and Collages

The project focuses on Mott Street, where fruit sellers use sidewalk space to showcase their goods using reused materials, including cardboard, crates, and styrofoam cartons, as their makeshift furniture. Inspired by this, this project tries to find out the connection between indoor activities adapted to outdoor public places using makeshift furniture while addressing the problem of waste abundance in the area.

Building and Repurposing

By utilizing discarded fabric from various sources, such as used clothing or upholstery scraps, we're reducing the demand for virgin materials and minimizing textile waste.

Repurposing plastic waste as a material addresses the issue of durability, particularity in wet weather conditions. Additionally, repurposing plastic prevents it from becoming thrown away in the landfill, contributing to environmental sustainability by giving discarded materials a new purpose.
